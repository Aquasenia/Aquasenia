Location dossier

Mirror Sea Reefs

Summary

The Mirror Sea Reefs are prismatic coral fields that reflect memories from the Crystal Age. Their luminous spires hold treaty echoes and historic images that surface during low-tide resonance windows. The reefs remain mostly shrouded, with guided access for scholars and diplomats.

Classification / Type

Location; oceanic reef sanctuary and memory archive.

Key Facts

  • Region: Mirror Sea.
  • Landmark type: Oceanic reef.
  • Status: Shrouded with limited, guided access.
  • Memory glints appear at dusk during calm tides.
  • Meralis guardians oversee entry windows.

Description

The reefs grow in mirrored strata, storing images of past moments when tides align with the Crystal Age resonance field. Resonant algae emit guidance lights, and coral gardens reshape after each aurora season.

History

During the Crystal Age, the reefs became ceremonial archives for treaty gatherings. After later resonance storms, the spires were closed to general travel, and Meralis guides now escort limited archival expeditions through low-tide corridors.
