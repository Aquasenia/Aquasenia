Species dossier

Meralis Tidemancers

Oceanic diplomats who braid water and light into memory threads, guiding treaty rites.

Lifespan 180–220 tidal cycles
Average height 1.7 meters
Average weight 74 kilograms
Relationship with humans Neutral
Species status Thriving
Overview

Meralis Tidemancers are tidebound emissaries who translate the Mirror Sea currents into living histories. Their ceremonial weaves are kept in the Archive Index for diplomatic reference.

Origin realm: Mirror Sea

Era discovered: Crystal Age

Primary role: Diplomats

Key traits
  • Water-light braiding rituals used to seal pacts.
  • Memory threads stored in coral spires beneath the Mirror Sea.
  • Renowned for calming interstellar disputes with harmonic tides.

Biology & Anatomy

Meralis physiology blends aquatic grace with bio-luminescent organs that pulse in synchrony with oceanic currents, aiding their memory-braiding rituals.

  • Skin tides shimmer with living pigment that records emotional resonance.
  • Gilled lungs allow long submersion while chanting treaty rites.
  • Vestigial fin crests help channel biolight through their braids.

Deities & Religions

The Tide Chorus venerates river-spirits and lunar patrons believed to guide the Mirror Sea. Offerings are released in light-braided shells during high tide.

  • Moonrise litanies bless treaty threads before each summit.
  • Priest-navigators read storm glyphs etched in tidal foam.
  • Harmony pools host quiet vigils for lost currents.

Background

The Tidemancers rose during the Crystal Age as treaty stewards, translating the Mirror Sea's memory currents into living histories that anchor interstellar accords.

  • First shoals brokered peace between coral citadels and surface fleets.
  • Memory braids are archived in the Index for diplomatic reference.
  • Historic pacts are retold during the Sea of Lanterns festival.

Society & Roles

Meralis society is organized around flowing kinships called shoals, each entrusted with a rotating role in treaty stewardship, memory keeping, and ritual hospitality.

  • Shoal councils gather at moonrise to harmonize seasonal vows.
  • Guests are welcomed through ceremonial tide circles and shared luminescent feasts.
  • Storykeepers braid biographies into water-light patterns for oral recall.
