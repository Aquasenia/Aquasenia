Species dossier

Meralis Tidemancers

Summary

The Meralis Tidemancers are oceanic diplomats from the Mirror Sea who braid water and light into memory threads. They calm disputes by weaving shared histories, then archive those agreements in coral spires. Their shoal councils rotate stewardship roles so every community remembers the treaties that hold the seas together.

Classification / Type

Species of amphibious treaty stewards and memory-braiders.

Key Facts

  • Lifespan ranges from 180–220 tidal cycles.
  • Average height is 1.7 meters with bioluminescent organs.
  • Memory threads are stored in coral spires beneath the Mirror Sea.
  • Diplomatic relationship with humans is neutral.
  • Primary role centers on treaty stewardship and mediation.

Description

Meralis physiology blends aquatic grace with gilled lungs and luminous fin crests. Their skin tides shimmer with pigments that record emotional resonance, allowing them to braid water-light memories that guide diplomatic rites.

History

The Tidemancers rose during the Crystal Age as treaty stewards, translating the Mirror Sea's memory currents into living histories. Early shoals brokered peace between coral citadels and surface fleets, and their memory braids remain the archive of record.
